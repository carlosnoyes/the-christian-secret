Chapter 15 Summary: Practical Results in the Daily Walk and Conversation
=========================================================================

**Main Idea**:
The life of faith must produce visible, practical results in daily living. Christians are God's witnesses to a world that reads lives rather than Bibles, and the inward life hid with Christ must manifest outwardly in Christlike character, selfless service, and a marked separation from worldly values and pursuits.

**Key Points**:
- The world judges Christianity not by its doctrines but by the transformed lives of its followers; in an age of facts, the Church must present actual evidence of God's power to change lives.
- The standard of practical holiness among most professing Christians is far too low; those called to consecration must live differently.
- Believers must not be conformed to the world in character, purpose, or lifestyle; their conversation must be "in Heaven."
- Specific practical marks are listed: meekness, quietness of spirit, submissive acceptance of God's will, sweetness under provocation, calmness amid turmoil, yieldingness, insensibility to slights, freedom from worry and fear.
- Consecrated Christians progressively give themselves to service, become indifferent to outward show, and dedicate their voice, purse, pen, lips, hands, and feet to God's use.
- The Holy Spirit guides believers step by step through the teachings and providences of daily life; obedience to each small prompting is essential.
- Yielding in small things develops the pliability God needs to mold believers into full conformity with Christ.
- Consecration means inevitable obedience -- the surrender of all liberty of choice and an hourly following of God's leading.
- The believer's part is to yield; God's part is to work. Every command He gives is accompanied by the power to obey it.
- The ultimate goal is to be "conformed to the image" of Christ -- not in bodily likeness but in character, thought, feeling, and action.
- Christ's Christianity teaches sacrifice to save others, in contrast to man's Christianity which teaches sacrifice to save ourselves.

**Illustrations and Examples**:
- The author's son at college: he wrote to a friend that the world will not read the Bible but will read Christian lives, making believers God's necessary witnesses.
- A soul led from darkness to blessing in three months: a woman who consecrated herself and obeyed each prompting of the Spirit was rapidly transformed, becoming a testimony to those around her, until she received the baptism of the Holy Spirit (Acts 1:5).
- A little girl's definition of being a Christian: "It means to be just what Christ would be, if He was a little girl and lived in my house."
- A thoughtful Hindu visitor to England and America: after observing Christianity, he concluded, "What Christians need is a little more of Christ's Christianity, and a little less of man's."
