Chapter 4 Summary: How to Enter In
====================================

Main Idea
---------
Entering the life of full trust requires exactly two steps: entire consecration
(abandonment of the whole self to God) and absolute faith (believing that God
accepts what is given and begins His work now). This life is not an attainment
earned by effort but an obtainment received as a gift.

Key Points
----------
- The blessed life is a gift of God, not an achievement; the receiver's only
  role is to take it and thank the Giver.
- Consecration (or abandonment) is the first step: placing spirit, soul, and
  body under God's absolute control, holding nothing back.
- Consecration is not legalistic or meritorious; it simply removes the
  obstacles so God can work.
- God's will is not something to fear but something to embrace; it is always
  the expression of His love and leads to blessing.
- Faith is the second step: believing that God has accepted our consecration
  and is now working in us, just as we once believed He forgave our sins.
- This faith must be present-tense -- "He saves me now" -- not a vague hope
  for future deliverance. The enemy delights in future faith because it
  produces no practical results.
- Just as we received Christ by faith for justification, we must now receive
  Him by faith for sanctification.
- These two steps, taken definitely and persevered in, will bring any believer
  into the green pastures and still waters of the higher life.

Illustrations and Examples
--------------------------
- The physician and the uncooperative patient: a doctor cannot cure a patient
  who refuses to reveal all symptoms or follow all prescriptions. God
  likewise requires that the whole case be put into His hands without
  reserves. The physician in the story grasped the point and committed
  himself to God on the spot.
- Little Charley and his mother: a Christian mother was afraid to say "Thy
  will be done." Her friend asked how she would feel if her son Charley said,
  "Mother, I will let you have your own way with me." She realized she would
  shower him with love, not hardship -- and that God is even more tender.
- A lady seeking to enter the life of trust asked her counselor to "do it out
  loud" so she could see how abandonment and faith are practically exercised.
  Smith provides a model prayer of surrender and trust.
- The man descending a well on a rope that seemed too short: in terror he let
  go, only to fall three inches to the bottom. The step of faith feels like
  a leap into the void but lands on solid rock.
- The "keep" in old English castles: the strongest, best-protected place where
  the weak and helpless hid in times of siege -- an image of hiding in God's
  keeping power.
