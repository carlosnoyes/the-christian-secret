Chapter 20 Summary: The Chariots of God
========================================

Main Idea
---------
Every trial, suffering, disappointment, and difficulty in life is actually one of
God's invisible chariots, sent to carry the soul to higher places of spiritual
victory. The choice before each believer is whether to lie down under trials and
be crushed, or to mount into them as chariots and ride triumphantly onward.

Key Points
----------
- God's chariots are invisible and "very many" (Psalm 68:17); they surround us
  on every side but are seen only by the eye of faith.
- What appears to be enemies, sufferings, defeats, and disappointments are
  actually chariots of triumph in disguise.
- Every event in life can be made either a Juggernaut car that crushes or a
  chariot that carries the soul to victory; it depends on how we receive it.
- Mounting into God's chariots produces a spiritual translation -- not into
  the heavens above, but into the heaven within, the "heavenly places in
  Christ Jesus."
- Every chariot sent by God is "paved with love," even when it comes through
  human malice, cruelty, or neglect.
- God must often destroy our own "chariots of Egypt" (the human
  instrumentalities we depend on) before we will mount into His chariots.
- We must come to the place of "He only" rather than "He and something else"
  before we can truly ride in God's chariots.
- To mount into the chariot: find God in the trial, hide yourself in Him,
  accept His will, say "Thy will be done," and shut out all second causes.

Illustrations and Examples
--------------------------
- Elisha and his servant (2 Kings 6:14-17): the servant saw only the Syrian
  army, but Elisha's prayer opened his eyes to see God's horses and chariots.
- Elijah's translation in a chariot of fire (2 Kings 2:11-12) as a picture of
  spiritual translation through trials.
- A lady with a very slow servant who had prayed for patience; she realized the
  slow servant was God's chariot to carry her into patience, and from that day
  she was never disturbed by anyone's slowness again.
- A sister at a convention forced to share a room with two talkative women;
  after hearing about God's chariots, she accepted them as her chariot to
  carry her into sweetness, then said, "Sisters, I am lying here riding in a
  chariot," which produced instant quiet.
- Joseph's path to his throne through slavery and prison -- his trials were the
  chariots that carried him to triumph.
- The baby carried in its mother's arms rides triumphantly through hard places
  without even knowing they are hard.
