Chapter 11 Summary: Difficulties Concerning Guidance
=====================================================

Main Idea
---------
The fully surrendered believer can confidently expect Divine guidance, but must
learn to discern God's voice by testing all impressions against four harmonizing
voices: Scripture, the inward impressions of the Holy Spirit, sanctified
judgment or common sense, and providential circumstances.

Key Points
----------
- Full surrender of the will is the prerequisite for discerning God's guidance;
  any reserve on any point makes it nearly impossible to know His mind on that
  point.
- Divine guidance is clearly promised in Scripture, and believers must settle
  this fact by faith before expecting to receive it.
- God may lead believers into paths that conflict with the wishes of even their
  dearest friends; one must be prepared for this while maintaining a spirit of
  love and gentleness.
- God speaks through four voices: (1) Scripture, (2) the inward impressions of
  the Holy Spirit, (3) our own higher judgment or common sense, and (4)
  providential circumstances.
- Where all four voices harmonize, it is safe to act; where they do not agree,
  one must wait until harmony is achieved.
- Other voices also speak to the soul -- the voice of self and the voices of
  deceiving spirits -- making discernment essential.
- The Bible is a book of principles, not isolated proof-texts; individual
  verses must be understood in the light of the whole counsel of Scripture.
- Sanctified common sense, enlightened by the Spirit, is a God-given faculty
  that must not be set aside in spiritual matters.
- If a leading is truly from God, providential circumstances will open the way;
  believers should never force doors open themselves.
- A "stop in the mind" must be respected; one should never act in doubt.
- God works His will by writing desires on our hearts, so that obedience feels
  like following our own wishes rather than obeying external commands.
- When the Spirit speaks, immediate obedience is the safest course; hesitation
  and reasoning make obedience progressively harder.

Illustrations and Examples
--------------------------
- The Christian woman at a Western meeting who blindfolded herself to "walk by
  faith" literally, stumbled into a stove and burned herself -- illustrating
  the danger of ignoring Scripture's principles and common sense.
- The Christian of "sound mind" who tested false spirits by seeing whether they
  had "any common-sense."
- A five-point practical rule set for guidance: believe God will guide, surrender
  your will, hearken for His voice, wait for harmony, obey without question.
- A closing poem (attributed in style to Frederick Faber) about keeping the
  conscience sensitive and catching the Spirit's words hour by hour.
