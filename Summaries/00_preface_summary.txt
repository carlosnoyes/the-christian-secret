Preface Summary: The Christian's Secret of a Happy Life
========================================================

Main Idea
---------
Hannah Whitall Smith introduces her book not as a theological treatise but as a
personal testimony of practical lessons God has taught her from His Word. She
writes out of her own experience of a happy Christian life, hoping to share her
secret so that other struggling believers may find the same happiness and rest.

Key Points
----------
- The book is experiential and practical rather than formally theological.
- Smith acknowledges she has no theological training and asks readers to look
  past any doctrinal imprecision to grasp the practical truth she offers.
- Her aim is to reach the absolute truth underlying all creeds and bring the
  soul into a personal relationship with God.
- She has committed the book to God, trusting Him to counteract any errors and
  allow only what is true to reach the reader's heart.
- The book is addressed specifically to "struggling, weary ones in the Church
  of Christ."

Illustrations and Examples
--------------------------
- No formal stories or analogies are used in the preface. Smith relies on a
  direct, heartfelt appeal to establish her purpose and tone.

References
----------
- No specific Bible verses are cited.
- The Holy Spirit is invoked as the teacher who will use the book.
- The author identifies herself as Hannah Whitall Smith of Germantown,
  Pennsylvania.
