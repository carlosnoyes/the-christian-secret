Chapter 2 Summary: The Scripturalness of This Life
====================================================

Main Idea
---------
Smith argues that Scripture clearly and repeatedly promises believers not only
forgiveness from the guilt of sin but full deliverance from its power in this
present life. The Higher Christian Life is not a new invention but a thoroughly
biblical reality confirmed by voices throughout church history.

Key Points
----------
- Many Christians experience a cycle of failure and repentance rather than the
  continual victory Scripture promises; this is not what Christ intended.
- Christ's death was meant to deliver believers from the power and dominion of
  sin, not merely from its guilt and future penalty.
- The Scriptures declare that God is "able to save unto the uttermost" and
  that His purpose is complete deliverance, not partial or limited rescue.
- It is inconsistent to believe that God, who hates sin, would arrange
  salvation in a way that makes freedom from sin's power impossible.
- This teaching is not novel; it has been proclaimed by saints and theologians
  throughout church history.
- Believers should search the Scriptures, trust God's power, and look away
  from their own weakness to the One who can deliver them.

Illustrations and Examples
--------------------------
- The contrast between a believer's initial shout of triumph at conversion and
  the subsequent reality of repeated defeats and discouragement.
- Dr. Chalmers' argument: it would be a "strange administration" for God to
  hate sin enough to impose death, yet tolerate sin in the redeemed.
- A 17th-century divine's analogy of the redeemed captive: paying ransom for a
  prisoner but leaving him in fetters for life would be considered a fraud;
  so too if Christ redeems us yet leaves us enslaved to sin.
- The analogy of a machine maker: if a maker declares his machine was built
  for a certain purpose yet it cannot perform that purpose, we would call him
  a fraud. Likewise God made man to have dominion and triumph.

References
----------
- Matthew 1:21 (angel to Joseph: "He shall save His people from their sins")
- Luke 1:68-75 (Zacharias' prophecy: delivered from enemies, serving God in
  holiness and righteousness all our days)
- Acts 3:26 (Peter: God sent Jesus "to bless you in turning away every one of
  you from his iniquities")
- Ephesians 5:25-27 (Christ gave Himself to sanctify the church, presenting
  it without spot or wrinkle)
- Titus 2:11-14 (grace teaches us to deny ungodliness; Christ redeems us from
  all iniquity)
- 1 Peter 2:21-24 (Christ bore our sins that we, being dead to sins, should
  live unto righteousness)
- Ephesians 4:22-24 (put off the old man, put on the new man created in
  righteousness and true holiness)
- Romans 6:1-6 (dead to sin, crucified with Christ, walk in newness of life)
- Romans 8:3-4 (God condemned sin in the flesh that the law's righteousness
  might be fulfilled in us)
- Psalm 8:6 (God made man to have dominion)
- 2 Corinthians 2:14 ("always causeth us to triumph in Christ")
- Ephesians 1:19-20 (the exceeding greatness of His power toward us)
- Romans 4:20-21 (Abraham, fully persuaded that God was able to perform)
- Hebrews 7:25 ("able to save to the uttermost")
- Dr. Thomas Chalmers (extended quotation on God's hatred of sin)
- An unnamed 17th-century Quaker divine (extended quotation on redemption
  from sin's power)
- Marshall and Romaine are mentioned as additional historical witnesses.
