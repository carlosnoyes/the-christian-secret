Chapter 1 Summary: God's Side and Man's Side
=============================================

Main Idea
---------
The Christian life of sanctification has two distinct sides that must be held
together: man's part is to trust, and God's part is to work. Confusion arises
when people hear only one side and mistake it for the whole picture, but both
sides are complementary rather than contradictory.

Key Points
----------
- Man's role is entirely one of surrender and trust; he places himself into
  God's hands and remains there by continual faith.
- God's role is to actively work on and in the believer, using inward exercises
  and outward providences to transform the soul into the image of Christ.
- Sanctification is both a sudden step of faith (man's side) and a gradual
  process of transformation (God's side).
- Purity of heart can be immediate and complete even in a new believer, but
  maturity of growth requires time and God's ongoing work.
- God's works are perfect at every stage of their growth; an apple in June is
  a perfect June apple, even though it differs from the perfected apple of
  October.
- The believer's part is to put himself into God's hands by faith and to keep
  himself there by a continuous exercise of faith.
- All efforts at self-transformation result in the "marring of the vessel";
  only God who created us can re-create us.

Illustrations and Examples
--------------------------
- Two preachers teaching alternate sides of the same truth, causing confusion
  in a listener who thought they contradicted each other.
- Two friends describing opposite sides of the same building and disputing
  until they realize they saw different facades.
- The potter and the clay: the clay's only job is to lie passive in the
  potter's hands; the potter does all the shaping, kneading, and firing.
- A saw in a carpenter's shop: the saw cuts the log, yet it is the carpenter
  who does the cutting through the instrument.
- A lady who received a great outpouring of the Spirit but was then set to
  domestic duties (sweeping, dusting, baking); she accepted the discipline
  and was made into a vessel fit for the Master's use.
- A second lady, placed with two peevish invalid nieces, who refused the
  training, lost her blessing, and went back into coldness and misery.
- The babe who perfectly pleases its mother even though far from maturity.
