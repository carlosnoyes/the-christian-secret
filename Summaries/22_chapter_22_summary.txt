Chapter 22 Summary: 'God with Us'; or, The One Hundred and Thirty-ninth Psalm
===============================================================================

Main Idea
---------
The chapter expounds on the truth of "Emmanuel, God with us" through Psalm 139,
demonstrating that God's continual presence with us is not a beautiful sentiment
but an incontrovertible fact. Truly believing this fact -- that God is always
present, always knowing, always caring -- is the solution to every difficulty in
life and the foundation of unwavering peace.

Key Points
----------
- God is not a far-off Deity but is living with us in the midst of our daily
  lives, as close to us as we are to ourselves; the Bible teaches this from
  cover to cover.
- We cannot escape God's presence: not in heaven, hell, the uttermost parts of
  the sea, nor in any darkness; He is not only with us but within us.
- Darkness may hide God from us, but it never hides us from God; the darkness
  and light are both alike to Him.
- God knows us completely: our downsitting and uprising, our thoughts, our
  words, all our ways. He understands us as no one else can, taking every
  element of our character into account.
- God "besets" us behind and before -- He goes after us to set straight what
  we have left crooked, like a mother picking up after her children.
- God Himself has presided over the creation of each person; our individual
  "make-up" is by His design, and we should be content with how He made us
  rather than wishing to be someone else.
- God's thoughts toward us are precious and more numerous than the sand; He
  pays attention to the smallest details of every life.
- The realized presence of God naturally drives out sin, as light drives out
  darkness.
- Practical steps: (1) convince yourself from Scripture that God's presence is
  a fact, (2) practice His presence always by acknowledging Him in all your
  ways, saying "The Lord is here" over each moment, and doing everything for
  His glory.

Illustrations and Examples
--------------------------
- The author's childhood memory: whenever her father or mother arrived saying
  "Daughter, I am here," every burden dropped and every anxiety was stilled;
  their simple presence was all the assurance she needed.
- A child in delirium crying out for its mother while the mother is already
  holding its hand and bathing its head -- the darkness of disease hides the
  mother from the child but not the child from the mother.
- A mother's care for her children: choosing their seats, making their beds
  soft, smoothing their paths, picking up after them, untangling their knots
  and kite-strings -- all paralleled with God's care for us.
- An art exhibition: no true artist repeats himself; each of us is a unique
  expression of God's creative power, and without any one of us, the
  exhibition would be incomplete.
- The man who makes an intricate machine is the best one to manage and repair
  it; likewise, God who made us is best suited to manage us.
- Old Betty, a poor ale-seller on a desolate English moor, who heard about
  God's presence, embraced it with simple faith, and upon arriving home
  immediately tipped her pot of ale into the road because "The Lord will not
  like to have that ale in the house where He lives."

References
----------

  is, God with us")
- Psalm 139:1-18, 21-24 (the central text of the chapter)
- Genesis (God's presence "amongst the trees of the garden")
- Exodus 25:8 ("Make me a sanctuary, that I may dwell among them")
- Matthew 28:20 ("Lo, I am with you alway, even unto the end of the world")
- Hebrews 13:5-6 ("I will never leave thee nor forsake thee... The Lord is
  my helper, and I will not fear...")
- 1 Corinthians 10:31 ("Whether therefore ye eat, or drink, or whatsoever ye
  do, do all to the glory of God")
- Psalm 16:11 ("In thy presence is fulness of joy...")
- Frederick William Faber, hymn: "There is no place where earth's sorrows /
  Are more felt than up in Heaven..."
- Faber, poem: "Thus doth thy hospitable greatness lie / Around us like a
  boundless sea..."
- An old Quaker writer: "Be content to be just what thy God has made thee"
- Brother Lawrence referenced indirectly through the phrase "practising His
  presence"
