Chapter 16 Summary: The Joy of Obedience
==========================================

**Main Idea**:
Perfect obedience becomes perfect happiness when we have perfect confidence in the One we obey. The chapter presents total surrender to God not as a burden but as the highest joy and privilege -- akin to the devotion of love rather than the mere duty of friendship. Those who yield themselves wholly to Christ discover an unspeakable delight that transforms even the most ordinary life.

**Key Points**:
- The saying "Perfect obedience would be perfect happiness, if only we had perfect confidence in the power we were obeying" captures the secret: Jesus is the Master to whom we can yield implicit obedience and find perfect rest.
- Many believers hesitate at complete surrender, willing to be "measurably obedient" but appalled by the thought of utter abandonment without reserves.
- Those who feel called to a stricter path than other Christians should see this not as a hardship but as a privilege of intimacy -- God reveals more of His mind to those He draws closer.
- The chapter distinguishes between friendship and love as an analogy: friendship allows separate interests and moderate devotion, but love requires total union where all belongs to both, and the wishes of one become binding obligations to the other.
- Those bound by love do not chafe under its obligations but glory in them and pity those who remain at a distance.
- God's love for the believer is compared to a bridegroom's love for his bride -- measureless devotion that asks for all in return.
- Christ asks each believer a series of searching questions: Will you follow into suffering? Accept no reward but my approval? Give me absolute control? Be content with pleasing me only?
- Surrendered obedience should extend to every detail of daily life -- time, talents, voice, intellect, reading, friendships, and pursuits.
- This life of devoted obedience brings a glory and enthusiasm that surpasses even the romance of youth, and it is available to the poorest and meanest lives alike.
- The joy is not one-sided: Scripture reveals that God Himself delights in those who surrender to Him and finds satisfaction in their love.
- The chapter closes with the blessings of Deuteronomy 28, applied spiritually to believers who hearken to God's voice.

**Illustrations and Examples**:
- The difference between friendship and love: friendship allows distance, separate interests, and moderate devotion; love demands total union, shared lives, and finds obligations to be sweet privileges -- used as an analogy for the soul's deepening relationship with God.
- The bridegroom and bride: God rejoices over the believer as a bridegroom over his bride, illustrating the intensity and intimacy of divine love.
- Alabaster boxes of ointment: the image of someone carrying precious love and devotion with no one close enough to pour it upon, until God offers Himself as that object.
- A poor woman at her wash-tub: even the most humble, monotonous life, when lived in Christ and following His leading, would be filled with an enthusiasm making every hour glorious.
