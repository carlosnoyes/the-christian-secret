Chapter 9 Summary: Growth
=========================

Main Idea
---------
True spiritual growth happens not by striving and self-effort but by being
planted in the grace of God and allowing the Divine Husbandman to cultivate
us. Believers must stop trying to grow into grace and instead learn to grow
in grace, resting in God's care as a lily rests in the sunshine.

Key Points
----------
- Many Christians try to "grow into grace" rather than "in it," which is like
  planting a rosebush on a stony path and expecting it to reach the garden.
- Growth in grace is opposed to all self-dependence, self-effort, and legality;
  it must be put entirely into God's hands.
- Real growth is the result of an inward life principle, not outward effort;
  just as a lily or a child grows without toiling or straining.
- The essential thing is to have the growing life -- the life hid with Christ
  in God, the indwelling Holy Spirit -- and growth will follow naturally.
- The believer's role is to abide in the Vine, remove barriers, and stay in
  the sunshine of God's presence; the Husbandman handles the cultivating,
  pruning, and all circumstances.
- God can transform any soil into the soil of grace; no past dwarfing,
  dryness, or crookedness can mar His perfect work if the soul surrenders.
- Believers should not try to be both the garden and the gardener; they must
  accept God's choice of plants, seasons, sunshine, and storms.

Illustrations and Examples
--------------------------
- The lady who had been "growing" for twenty-five years yet was less devoted
  than at the start of her Christian life -- illustrating failed self-effort.
- The rosebush planted on a stony path, trying to grow into the flower bed.
- The children of Israel wandering forty years in the wilderness without
  gaining an inch of the Promised Land, versus their rapid conquest once they
  entered it.
- The lilies of the field that toil not, neither do they spin, yet surpass
  Solomon's glory -- Jesus's teaching on effortless growth.
- A child with a monomania for growing who rigs ropes and pulleys to stretch
  himself taller, accomplishing nothing.
- A lily trying to manage clouds and sunshine to clothe itself.
- The violet resting contentedly in its little nook.
- A poem about "the tree God plants" that no wind, frost, or storm can harm.
