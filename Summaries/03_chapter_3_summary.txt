Chapter 3 Summary: The Life Defined
=====================================

Main Idea
---------
The Higher Christian Life consists in letting the Lord carry all our burdens
and manage all our affairs -- both outward and inward -- instead of trying to
do it ourselves. It is, in essence, living as a carefree child in the Father's
house, resting in His love and provision.

Key Points
----------
- The life hid with Christ in God is simply handing over every burden to the
  Lord and leaving it there.
- The greatest burden we carry is ourselves: our temperaments, weaknesses,
  feelings, and inward struggles. This must be the first thing surrendered.
- We must also lay off every outward burden: health, reputation, work,
  children, business, and all circumstances.
- Most Christians trust God with their eternal souls but insist on managing
  their daily lives themselves, as if it were too much to ask God to carry
  those burdens too.
- The secret is not merely taking burdens to God but leaving them with Him
  and not bringing them back.
- Obeying "Be careful for nothing" (Philippians 4:6) produces the promised
  result: "the peace of God which passeth all understanding."
- The command covers everything -- "In NOTHING be anxious" -- with no
  exceptions for spiritual concerns or temporal ones.

Illustrations and Examples
--------------------------
- The man riding in a wagon who kept his heavy burden on his shoulders instead
  of setting it down, because he felt it was too much to ask the driver to
  carry both him and his load.
- "Hannah's Faith": a tract about a poor woman who carried her sorrows
  triumphantly because she took them to the Lord and left them there. A
  Christian lady read this tract and adopted the practice, finding perfect
  peace even though her circumstances did not change.
- The analogy of going to bed at night: relaxing every muscle and trusting the
  bed to hold you up, versus lying rigid with doubt about whether the bed
  will collapse.
- The child-life: a little child lives by faith, provides nothing for himself,
  takes no thought for the morrow, and rests in his father's care with
  complete unconcern and perfect ease.
- An adopted child in a wealthy home, running in and out free and
  light-hearted -- a picture of our position in our Heavenly Father's house.
- The sparrows and lilies of the field (Matthew 6:25-34) as models of
  anxiety-free living under God's care.
- A poem: "I am only a tiny sparrow," celebrating the sparrow's trust in God's
  watchful provision.

References
----------
- Colossians 3:3 ("life hid with Christ in God")
- 2 Timothy 2:21 ("sanctified, and meet for the Master's use")
- Philippians 4:6-7 ("Be careful for nothing... the peace of God which

- Matthew 6:25-34 (take no thought; behold the fowls and the lilies)
- Matthew 18:2-3 ("Except ye be converted and become as little children")
