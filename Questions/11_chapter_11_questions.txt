Chapter 11: Difficulties Concerning Guidance — Discussion Questions
====================================================================

1. Smith identifies four voices through which God speaks — Scripture, inward impressions of the Holy Spirit, sanctified judgment (common sense), and providential circumstances — and says all four must harmonize before we act. In your experience, what do you do when three of the four seem to agree but one does not — and which of the four do you tend to weight most heavily?

2. The chapter warns that "evil and deceiving spirits" can also make inward impressions on our souls that feel very much like the voice of God. How do you practically distinguish between a genuine prompting of the Holy Spirit and an impression that comes from your own desires, fears, or even spiritual deception?

3. Smith tells of a woman who took the text "we walk by faith, not by sight" as a literal command to blindfold herself and walk around — and was burned by a stove. The lesson is that Scripture must be read as a book of principles, not isolated proof-texts. Can you think of ways Christians today similarly misapply isolated verses, and what safeguards does Smith's four-voice framework provide against this?

4. The chapter says God's guidance usually comes not as external commands but as "desires springing up within" — we feel as though we *want* to do what God wills. How do you tell the difference between a God-given desire and one that is merely your own preference dressed up in spiritual language?

5. Smith says that when the Spirit speaks, "it is always easy to obey" in that moment, but if the soul hesitates and begins to reason, obedience becomes increasingly difficult. Do you find this true in your experience — and how do you balance the call to act promptly on conviction with the equally important call to test impressions carefully before acting?
