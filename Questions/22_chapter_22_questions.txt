Chapter 22: 'God with Us' — Discussion Questions
==================================================

1. Smith recalls that as a child, the simple announcement "Daughter, I am here" from her father or mother was enough to make every burden drop and every anxiety still — not because of any promise they made, but simply because of their presence. What would change in your daily life if the words "God is here" carried the same weight for you that a parent's arrival carried for that child?

2. The Psalm declares that God's presence is inescapable — in heaven, in hell, in the uttermost parts of the sea, in the deepest darkness. Smith says the problem is not that God is absent but that we do not see Him. What blinds us to a presence that Scripture says is literally everywhere, and what "opens our eyes" to it?

3. Smith addresses a struggle many people quietly carry: dissatisfaction with the way they have been "put together" — their temperament, their natural characteristics, their peculiar make-up. She says this is really a complaint against the Creator, and quotes an old Quaker: "Be content to be just what thy God has made thee." How do you hold this contentment together with the genuine call to grow and be transformed — where is the line between accepting how God made you and refusing to stay as you are?

4. Old Betty, the poor ale-seller on the English moor, grasped the reality of God's presence and immediately tipped her pot of ale into the road, saying "The Lord will not like to have that ale in the house where He lives." Then she prayed, "If there is anything else in this cottage that thee does not like, show it to me, and it shall be tipped out too." What is the "pot of ale" in your life — the thing you already know does not belong in a house where God lives — and what would it take to tip it out?

5. The book ends where it began — with the simple practice of trust. Smith's final counsel is to "practise His presence" through continual, simple acknowledgment: "The Lord is here." No elaborate meditations, no great efforts, just an unwavering recognition repeated throughout the day. As you close this book, what is the one truth from the entire journey that you most need to carry with you, and how will you practice it starting tomorrow?
