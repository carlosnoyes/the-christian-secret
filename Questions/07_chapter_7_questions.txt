Chapter 7: Difficulties Concerning the Will — Discussion Questions
===================================================================

1. Smith draws a sharp distinction between the will and the emotions, insisting that "the will is king" and the emotions are merely servants. Yet modern psychology often treats emotions as carrying vital information about our inner state. How do you navigate between Smith's counsel to disregard emotional clamoring and the recognition that emotions sometimes reveal things the will has not yet acknowledged?

2. The young man enslaved by habitual doubt was told to simply put his will on the believing side and repeat "I will believe, I do believe" until his emotions followed. Some might call this self-deception or suppression; Smith calls it the path to freedom. What is the difference between choosing to believe against your feelings and merely pretending — and how can you tell which you are doing?

3. William Hill's remarkable letter describes making his covenant of consecration "intellectually without any change in my feeling, with a heart full of hardness and darkness, unbelief and sin and insensibility." Most of us would wait for a heart that felt ready. What does it say about the nature of spiritual commitment that it can begin in such barren conditions — and does that challenge or encourage you?

4. Smith compares the will to "a wise mother in a nursery" and the feelings to "a set of clamoring, crying children." The mother does not let the children govern, but she also does not ignore them — she tries to "soothe and quiet them." What does it look like practically to acknowledge your emotions without being governed by them in the life of faith?

5. Hill writes that he "hit upon the method of living by the moment" — refusing to let the adversary trouble him about past or future, and looking for grace only for the present instant. In a culture obsessed with long-range planning and self-improvement goals, what would a genuinely moment-by-moment dependence on God actually look like in your daily life, and what would you have to give up to live that way?
